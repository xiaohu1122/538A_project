Seoul Bike Sharing Demand Analysis¶

Group 2: Xiao Hu, Vinky Wang, Izzy Zhang

Introduction¶

The bike-sharing system has made public transportation cleaner and more convenient as citizens can get access to bicycles across the city. As of 2020, 3200 bikes were being offered in Seoul, South Korea [1]. However, imbalance in demand and bicycles supplied can create problems for both users and system operators. Predicting bicycle usage helps guide companies to control the amount of bikes to deploy. In our project, we will be using different environmental variables and time as predictors for the hourly count of bike rentals.

The dataset was collected by the website of “South Korea Public Holidays” [2] and used by Sathishkumar et al. to test different data mining techniques and to make inferences and predictions [1].

As a general rule of thumb, a variance inflation factor of 5 is used to select for variables that have zero to moderate correlation [4]. Based on the correlation plot and variance inflation factor, we notice that temperature and dew are quite correlated with one another. Additionally, the variance inflation factor for humidity (>5) suggests high correlation with other variables. We removed the dew point variable which reduced the correlation between temperature and humidity since dew point is the temperature to which air must be cooled to become saturated with water vapor and is affected by humidity.

Methods: Plan¶

Our report will be trustworthy since the chosen dataset is provided by an Open Data Portal in Seoul. It is a recent dataset with 8465 records collected in 2020 and used by the other literature. In order to ensure our model is robust, we will check the model assumptions, including linearity, homoscedasticity, independence, and normality, by diagnostic plots such as residual plots and Q-Q plots. We will also be careful with the multicollinearity problem and check the correlation coefficient between covariates while selecting variables.

Since our response variable is the number of rented bikes per hour, we will build a Poisson regression model to predict the demand for rental bikes in Seoul. The model can be written as:

$$\log (E[Y|X]) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 X_1 + \beta_2 X_2 + ... + \beta_p X_p$$

where $Y \sim Pois(\mu)$ and $E[Y|X] = \mu$.

We will split the dataset into training data and test data. The training data will be used to select and fit the predictive model. Because Lasso regularization can reduce the model complexicity and prevent overfitting, we will use Lasso regularization to obtain the optimal subsets of input variables and evaluate the goodness of fit of the model by deviance and likelihood ratio test. After variable selection, we will use the trained model to predict the response of the test set and evaluate the prediction accuracy by root-mean-square error.

In the final report, We expect to see a significant association between the number of rented bikes and explanatory variables. We aim to obtain a precise prediction of rental demand from our regularized Poisson regression model. Our model could be used to predict the demand for rental bikes in Seoul on both working days and holidays under different weather conditions. This report could be helpful for bicycle rentals to provide a stable supply and for the government or urban planners to manage public bicycle traffic.
